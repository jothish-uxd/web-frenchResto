Années de passion
pour le produit juste

Chef Aurélien Marchand trained under three Michelin-starred kitchens before opening Maison Colette in 2009. His philosophy is unchanged: buy from farmers he knows, cook what is perfect today, and let simplicity be the boldest statement on the plate.

"Un bon repas est une chose simple, rendue extraordinaire par l'attention portée à chaque détail."

Accords du Sommelier

Three selections chosen by our sommelier Léa Fontaine to accompany the new spring menu.

Bourgogne Blanc

Meursault 1er Cru

Domaine Leflaive · 2021

Pale gold with a fine bead. Notes of toasted almond, white peach, and warm brioche. A long minerally finish with the salinity of Puligny soil.

Pairing Turbot de ligne · Asperges blanches
Loire · Sancerre

Sancerre Blanc

Henri Bourgeois · 2022

Bright and precise, with gooseberry, cut grass, and flint. The freshness cuts beautifully through the richness of the crab and the citrus in the amuse.

Pairing Tourteau breton · Amuse-bouche
Bourgogne Rouge

Gevrey-Chambertin

Rossignol-Trapet · 2019

Deep ruby with garnet hues. Wild cherry, iron, and dried herbs. The tannins are silky yet firm — built to carry the delicate fat of milk-fed lamb.

Pairing Agneau de lait · Plateau de fromages
